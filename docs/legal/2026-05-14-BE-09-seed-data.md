## What this change touches

BE-09 adds `prisma/seed.ts` — an idempotent seed script that upserts 1 admin, 3 doctors, 2 RMOs, 1 reception, 3 departments, and 10 demo patient rows. Demo users get a fixed bcrypt-hashed password `Demo@123` (cost 10). The script is gated to non-production usage by convention (`README` note and a TODO in the source) but is **not** technically prevented from running against a prod `DATABASE_URL`.

The seeded Patient rows include synthetic full names, fake phone numbers, and synthetic Indian addresses — no real PHI.

## Why it matters for legal review

- **Synthetic PHI only.** No real patient data is included. The data-fiduciary obligation does not engage here, because none of these records are real people.
- **Demo credentials are public.** `dr.yuvraaj@example.com / Demo@123` is documented in the seed file. Acceptable for local dev and the May-28 demo on the dev environment; must not be seeded into production. A separate task should add a hard guard (`if (process.env.NODE_ENV === "production") throw ...`) before any clinic-controlled environment exists.

## Recommendation for Aman

Low concern. The only follow-up is the production-guard on the seed runner — that's an engineering follow-up, not a legal blocker. No DPDP/consent surface is touched. Merge approved under Sprint-1 advisory mode.
